About Queen Bee

Here at Queen Bee we are on a quest to save the bees by promoting beekeeping and organic honey.
   All our honey comes from our farm and is produced by the busy bees we keep. They use a range of flowers including lavender, thistles and wildflowers. Our bee keepers gather honey four times a year. We proudly follow standards set by the British Bee Association. Our bee-hives provide a home for thousands of honey bees. We are a family run business that we started just five years ago, so we hope you will support us by coming to our farm cafe, bee-keeping courses, or buying honey in our shop.

Bee facts:

🐝There are over 250 species of bees in the Uk, including the Honey-bee which normally lives in hives managed by bee keepers.
🌼We can help bees by planting more traditional cottage flowers such as primrose, marigold, buddleia and wildflowers.
🌱Our econmomy and food prodcution relies on bees as a main pollinator of crops.
🌎Some bee species are in decline because of habitat loss and climate change.


Bee Keeping Courses

INTRODUCTION TO BEE-KEEPING

This one-day course is designed as an introduction for potential new beekeepers or beekeepers with less than 2 years’ experience.
The morning is a classroom lecture, and the afternoon is a practical demonstration within our hives to allow you "hands on" experience.
We will cover all aspects of what you should be looking for at your hive inspection so that you understand the life cycle and learn to “read” your bees.
Protective clothing will be supplied if required but please bring stout boots or "wellies” and a packed lunch
🍯🐝Course will run 10am till 4pm
